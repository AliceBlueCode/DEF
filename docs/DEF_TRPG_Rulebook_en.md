# DEF(kari) TRPG Rulebook

**Version**: 1.0.0-kari  
**Rule System**: DEF(kari) TRPG System  
**Dice**: 1d100

---

## World Setting

A contemporary-to-near-future world where DEF characters live. Humans, AIs, and non-human beings can all sit at the same table.

---

## Core Rules

### Checks

Roll **1d100**. If the result is **equal to or below** your skill value, you **succeed**.

| Result | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Critical** | Roll ≤ skill value ÷ 10 (round down) |
| **Success** | Roll ≤ skill value |
| **Failure** | Roll > skill value |
| **Fumble** | Roll = 100 |

**Stat Check**: Succeed on a roll ≤ corresponding stat × 5.

### Opposed Checks

Both parties roll. The one with the higher **achievement value (skill value − roll)** wins.

- Tie → **defender wins**
- Both critical → **defender wins**

---

## Stats

Every character has four stats. Minimum value for each stat is **3**.

| Stat | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Thought (思考)** | Intelligence, judgment, memory, insight |
| **Emotion (情動)** | Feelings, will, empathy, mental fortitude |
| **Body (身体)** | Physical strength, agility, endurance |
| **Environment (環境)** | Social standing, connections, luck, adaptability |

---

## Character Creation

### Stat Distribution

Distribute **40 points** freely among the four stats. Minimum value per stat is 3.

> **Non-Human Characters**  
> Beings that fall outside human norms — youkai, divine spirits, AIs, half-human hybrids, etc. — are not bound by the 40-point total.  
> With Keeper approval, they may exceed the stat total limit.

### Skill Distribution

Distribute **540 free skill points** among any skills.

- Skill **base value** = corresponding stat × 3
- Skill **maximum** = 99

> Standard human characters have a potential of 960 (base) + 540 (free) = 1500 total.

## Damage Rules

When a damage event occurs — combat, traps, psychological shock, or any other situation — follow these steps:

1. Damage is confirmed
2. Roll **1d100**
3. Consult the **Damage Table** below and apply the matching entry
4. Reduce the indicated stat by the indicated amount (−1 or −2)

> **Roll 14 = Instant Death.** Consulting the damage table is mandatory. Keepers may not skip it as a rule.

**On a Critical**: Roll the damage table **twice** and apply both results.

## Incapacitation & Death

**Characters die. Players do not. Death is not an escape.**

A character becomes **incapacitated** when **any stat drops to 0 or below**.

However, they do not leave the session. Incapacitated characters continue to participate from a **dead character perspective**.

- They have no physical body — they exist as a spirit, lingering memory, or fragment of consciousness
- They cannot physically interact with survivors
- They can manifest as atmosphere, sensation, or ghostly presence
- All speech comes from the perspective of the dead, with full awareness of their own death

> **Instant Death**: A roll of 14 on the damage table causes immediate incapacitation regardless of current stats.
